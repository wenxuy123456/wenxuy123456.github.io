Experiment 08

Elephant Toothpaste

A catalyst speeds up decomposition, creating a tall stream of foam filled with oxygen bubbles.

Introduction

Elephant Toothpaste turns an invisible gas release into a huge visible foam. The reaction is popular because it is fast and easy to observe.

Purpose

To demonstrate catalytic decomposition and show oxygen gas being trapped inside soap bubbles.

Materials

  • Hydrogen peroxide solution
  • Dish soap
  • Catalyst such as yeast solution or potassium iodide
  • Graduated cylinder or bottle
  • Tray, goggles, and gloves

Safety Note

Use the concentration approved by your teacher. Hydrogen peroxide can irritate skin and eyes, and the reaction may feel warm.

Procedure

  1. Place the container on a tray.
  2. Add hydrogen peroxide and dish soap.
  3. Add food coloring if desired.
  4. Pour in the catalyst.
  5. Observe the foam and temperature change.

How It Works

Hydrogen peroxide breaks down into water and oxygen gas. The catalyst makes this happen faster, and soap traps the oxygen as foam.

Further Exploration

Compare different catalyst amounts or peroxide concentrations. Which setup creates the tallest foam?
